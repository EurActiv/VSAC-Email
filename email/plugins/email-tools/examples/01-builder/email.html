Burkini ban brings difficult questions

By James Crisp

France’s ban on the burkini today led to some tough questions for the European Commission.

Mindful of the risk of getting caught up in a controversy that today sparked protests outside the French embassy in London, spokesman Alexander Winterstein stonewalled reporters’ questions over the morality of the ban but did reveal there were no rules stopping an EU official from coming to work in a burka.

He rightly pointed out that such policy decisions are not an EU competence, but the Commission has not always been so shy in coming forward on questions of ethics.

It certainly has not held back from raising its concerns over the state of democracy in Poland or just yesterday dancing around the edges of commenting on a criminal case in Greece.

Is this, some wondered, a case of one rule for France and another for less influential member states?
